Effective and efficient incident management procedures

Introduction

Effective and efficient incident management involves a formal process to detect, analyze, contain, eradicate, recover, and conduct post-incident activities. This runbook provides detailed procedures that govern the incident management process to handle data breach incidents.

Incident Assessment Methodology

The incident assessment methodology consists of the evaluation of impact, scope, and threat escalation.

Impact

The evaluation of the effects of a data breach on business functions, data, and recovery efforts. Factors include current and future functional impact, informational impact (confidentiality, integrity, availability), and the time/resources required for recovery.

Rating Definition
High Exposes highly sensitive/regulated data (PII, PHI, PCI); likely to impose major costs and interrupt business continuity.
Medium Exposes internal/confidential data; damaging but no regulatory or legal implications.
Low Exposes data that is otherwise public; the manner obtained warrants a security incident.

Scope

The evaluation of the effects on information technology (IT) systems. Scope is a critical component for decision-making.

Rating Definition
High ≥100 records exfiltrated
Medium 11-99 records exfiltrated
Low ≤10 records exfiltrated

Threat Escalation Protocol

This protocol defines the stakeholders needed during the incident management process. Informing and consulting these stakeholders is crucial when defending against a data breach.

Impact / Scope High Medium Low
High Tier 1 Tier 1 Tier 2
Medium Tier 1 Tier 2 Tier 2
Low Tier 2 Tier 2 Tier 3

Stakeholder Definition

Tier Stakeholders
Tier 1 End User, Help Desk, Cybersecurity, IT Operations, CISO, Legal, HR, PR, Senior Management, External Third Parties
Tier 2 End User, Help Desk, Cybersecurity, IT Operations, CISO
Tier 3 End User, Help Desk, Cybersecurity

Incident Management Summary

Question Details
What is the incident? A data breach occurs when records are exposed through internal error or attack. Commonly involves PII, but loss of such data is not required for a breach to occur.
Why should we care? A breach can cause huge financial and reputational damage and legal implications.
How do we respond? Stakeholders defined in the escalation protocol work collaboratively across major activities (monitoring, analysis, isolation, reporting).

Response Procedures

Detection Phase

Teams evaluate potential incidents, such as suspicious log activity or user reports.

Team Action
End User Report known or suspected breach of data.
Help Desk Open a help desk ticket.
Cybersecurity Monitor systems and open incident records as appropriate.

Analysis Phase

Teams determine the impact of the threat to guide remediation and escalation.

Team Action
Cybersecurity Analyze logs, identify points of failure, determine business impact, and notify CISO if significant.
IT Operations Review server logs to determine attack vector and exposed vulnerabilities.
Legal/HR/PR Evaluate regulatory compliance (GDPR/HIPAA) and reputational damage.

Containment Phase

Isolate affected systems and block attacker access to the network.

Team Action
Cybersecurity Disable breached accounts and update system passwords.
IT Operations Close unnecessary ports and block malicious IPs.
Legal/HR/PR Notify affected stakeholders as per GDPR/PIPEDA requirements.

Eradication Phase

Eliminate incident components (malware) and mitigate exploited vulnerabilities.

Team Action
IT Operations Patch systems and address vulnerability gaps.
CISO Approve new security controls and strategies.
Senior Management Allocate budget and resources for eradication.

Recovery Phase

Restore systems to normal operation and confirm full functionality.

Team Action
Cybersecurity Validate systems are back to normal and update incident record.
IT Operations Restore data and scan systems for normal operations.

Post-Incident Phase

Perform root-cause analysis and lessons-learned activities for continuous improvement.

Team Action
CISO Facilitate post-incident meetings and lessons-learned sessions.
Cybersecurity Document all findings in a final incident report.
Legal/HR/PR Update policies based on lessons learned.
