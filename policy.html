Incident Response Policy
Global Security & Risk — Incident Handling and Reporting Framework

Overview

An incident, as defined by NIST SP 800-61, is a violation or imminent threat of violation of computer security policies or practices. This policy defines the procedures for identifying, responding to, and managing cybersecurity incidents.

Objective

Ensure the organisation is prepared to detect, respond to, and recover from cybersecurity incidents while protecting systems, data, and operational continuity.

Scope

This policy applies to all systems, employees, and third-party services operating under NIS2 jurisdiction.

Roles & Responsibilities

Incident Manager

An Incident Manager leads the response to security incidents like breaches, or system compromises. They coordinate with technical teams to contain the threat and resolve it. They do this through communication with management and stakeholders to minimise the financial damage.

Technical Lead

A Technical Lead gives technical direction for security operations. They guide analyst and engineers to make key implemnetation decisions and to ensure solutions follow the best practises and security standards. Alongside hands on technical work they also mentor team members and act as a main technical point between the team and management.

Communications Officer

A Communications Officer looks after internal and external messaging during security incidents. They ensure there are clear and accurate updates being shared with the stakeholders and leadership. During incidents they coordinate with the technical and legal teams to deliver consistent timely communications that help maintain an organisations transparency and compliance.

Incident Detection Methods

  • SIEM monitoring (Wazuh)
  • Windows Defender alerts
  • System event logs
  • User-reported suspicious activity

Incident Classification

  • Low: No operational impact
  • Medium: Suspicious activity requiring investigation
  • High: Confirmed compromise
  • Critical: Major service or data breach

Incident Response Lifecycle

  • Detection
  • Analysis
  • Containment
  • Eradication
  • Recovery
  • Post-Incident Review

NIS2 Reporting Requirements

24-Hour Early Warning

  • Basic incident description
  • Affected systems
  • Initial mitigation actions

72-Hour Detailed Report

  • Full incident timeline
  • Impact assessment
  • Root cause analysis
  • Remediation actions

Evidence Retention

Security logs and incident records must be retained for at least 90 days.

Policy Review

This policy will be reviewed annually or after any major incident.

Compliance

  • Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action
  • All staff must cooperate during investigations
