Security Incident Management Runbook: Phishing Attack
Procedures for detecting, analysing, and responding to phishing attacks

Revision History

Version Change Author(s) Date
1.0

Introduction

Phishing attacks involve malicious emails designed to trick users into revealing sensitive information such as login credentials, financial data, or corporate information. These attacks may contain malicious links, attachments, or fraudulent requests.

This runbook outlines the procedures required to detect, analyse, contain, eradicate, recover from, and review phishing incidents.

Incident Management Summary

Question Details
What is the incident? Phishing emails impersonating trusted organisations to steal credentials or distribute malware.
Why should we care? Phishing is one of the most common attack vectors and can lead to credential compromise, data breaches, or ransomware.
How do we respond? Security teams detect suspicious emails, investigate the scope of the attack, isolate affected accounts, and remove malicious emails.

Response Procedures

Detection Phase

Team Action
End User Report suspicious emails, unusual login alerts, or unexpected credential requests.
Help Desk Create an incident ticket and collect details about the suspicious email.
Cybersecurity Monitor email security gateway alerts, SIEM logs, and phishing reports.

Analysis Phase

Team Action
Cybersecurity Analyse email headers, links, and attachments to identify phishing indicators.
IT Operations Review email server logs and determine how many users received the phishing email.
CISO Assess business impact and determine escalation level.

Containment Phase

Team Action
Cybersecurity Block malicious domains, IP addresses, and sender accounts.
IT Operations Remove phishing emails from affected inboxes.
Help Desk Notify affected users and instruct them not to interact with the email.

Eradication Phase

Team Action
IT Operations Reset compromised passwords and enforce MFA where required.
Cybersecurity Perform malware scans on any affected endpoints.
CISO Approve security improvements and policy updates.

Recovery Phase

Team Action
IT Operations Restore affected systems and validate user accounts.
Cybersecurity Monitor for further phishing attempts.
Business Units Confirm normal operations have resumed.

Post-Incident Phase

Team Action
CISO Conduct lessons-learned meeting.
Cybersecurity Document findings and update detection rules.
HR / Training Deliver phishing awareness training to employees.
