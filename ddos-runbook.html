Security Incident Management Runbook: DDoS Attack
Procedures for detecting and mitigating Distributed Denial of Service attacks

Revision History

Version Change Author(s) Date
1.0

Introduction

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack attempts to disrupt normal access to services by overwhelming systems with excessive traffic. These attacks may target web applications, APIs, network infrastructure, or cloud services.

This runbook outlines procedures for identifying, analysing, containing, and recovering from DDoS incidents to restore service availability.

Incident Management Summary

Question Details
What is the incident? Malicious traffic designed to overwhelm services and prevent legitimate users from accessing systems.
Why should we care? DDoS attacks can disrupt services, damage reputation, and lead to financial losses.
How do we respond? Security teams monitor abnormal traffic patterns, implement traffic filtering, scale infrastructure, and restore normal service operations.

Response Procedures

Detection Phase

Team Action
Monitoring Systems Detect abnormal traffic spikes or service degradation through monitoring alerts.
Help Desk Log reports from users experiencing slow or unavailable services.
Cybersecurity Review network traffic, firewall alerts, and SIEM logs to confirm DDoS activity.

Analysis Phase

Team Action
Cybersecurity Analyse traffic patterns to determine attack vector (HTTP flood, SYN flood, UDP reflection).
IT Operations Review server and load balancer performance metrics.
CISO Assess impact and determine escalation level.

Containment Phase

Team Action
Cybersecurity Apply firewall or WAF rules to block malicious IP ranges.
IT Operations Enable rate limiting and traffic filtering.
Network Team Activate DDoS protection services (e.g., CDN or cloud protection services).

Eradication Phase

Team Action
Cybersecurity Identify attack sources and block malicious domains, IP addresses, and networks.
IT Operations Scale infrastructure resources to maintain service availability.
CISO Approve additional mitigation measures and resource allocation.

Recovery Phase

Team Action
IT Operations Confirm service availability and system stability.
Cybersecurity Monitor traffic patterns for recurring attacks.
Business Units Verify systems are functioning normally for users.

Post-Incident Phase

Team Action
CISO Conduct post-incident review and lessons learned.
Cybersecurity Update detection rules and monitoring alerts.
IT Operations Improve infrastructure resilience and scaling policies.
